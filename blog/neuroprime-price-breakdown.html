Why Premium Supplements Cost More

Before dissecting the specific packages, it is necessary to establish why a product like NeuroPrime costs significantly more than a generic multivitamin found in a large retail pharmacy. The discrepancy lies entirely in the sourcing and standardization of the active ingredients.

Generic supplements frequently use synthetic, poorly absorbed forms of vitamins (like magnesium oxide) purely to lower manufacturing costs. They also rarely standardize their botanical extracts, meaning one batch of an herb might be potent, while the next is essentially inert plant fiber.

A formula aiming to legitimately influence cerebral blood flow and neurotransmitter synthesis requires high-quality choline donors (like Alpha GPC) and standardized extracts of Bacopa Monnieri and Ginkgo Biloba. These raw materials are expensive to source, process, and test for heavy metals within the USA. If a brain supplement costs ten dollars a bottle, it is virtually guaranteed to be an under-dosed placebo.
